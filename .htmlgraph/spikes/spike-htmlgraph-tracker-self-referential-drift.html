During feature-self-tracking development, the HtmlGraph drift detection system identified 5 high-drift activities (0.71 score). Analysis reveals these activities are the system investigating its own drift tracking mechanism - a meta-circular problem.

The Meta-Circular Problem:

The system reads/writes .htmlgraph/* infrastructure files to classify and document that it's reading/writing .htmlgraph/* infrastructure files. This creates a detection signal that must be distinguished from actual off-topic work.

Key Questions to Investigate:

  1. Should infrastructure operations (.htmlgraph/* reads/writes) contribute to drift scoring?
  2. How can we distinguish between "legitimate meta-work" and "actual drift"?
  3. What filtering strategy prevents false positives while maintaining drift detection?
  4. Should different agents have different drift tolerances?
  5. How do we handle the recursive nature of self-tracking?
